01 of 04

Wisconsin's turnout landscape

Wisconsin's 72 counties span a huge range — from Dane County (Madison) with a highly educated urban population, to sparse rural counties in the north.

Statewide, 2020 turnout was among the highest in the nation. But that number hides enormous local variation.

02 of 04

Not everywhere kept up

Even in a record year, roughly 18 counties fell below 60% turnout. They're not scattered randomly — they cluster geographically in the north and west.

What do these communities share?

03 of 04

Wealthier counties vote more

Sorting Wisconsin counties into income quartiles reveals a near-linear pattern: the wealthier the county, the higher its average voter turnout.

The gap between the top and bottom quartile is roughly 25 percentage points — a chasm, not a blip.

04 of 04

The county-level picture

Each dot is a county. The trend confirms what the bars suggested: as median household income rises, voter turnout rises with it.

Income alone doesn't explain everything — but it's a powerful predictor. Up next: education.
