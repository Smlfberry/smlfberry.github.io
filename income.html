Counties Below 60% Turnout
01 of 04

Illinois's turnout landscape

Illinois's 102 counties span a wide range — from wealthy suburban counties outside Chicago to low-income rural counties in the south.

Statewide, 2024 turnout was below the national average. But that number hides enormous local variation.

02 of 04

Not everywhere kept up

Roughly 21 counties fell below 60% turnout. They're not scattered randomly — they cluster geographically in the north and west.

What do these communities share?

03 of 04

Wealthier counties vote more

Sorting Illinois counties into income quartiles reveals a near-linear pattern: the wealthier the county, the higher its average voter turnout.

The gap between the top and bottom quartile is roughly 7.5 percentage points — a chasm, not a blip.

04 of 04

The county-level picture

Each dot is a county. The trend confirms what the bars suggested: as median household income rises, voter turnout rises with it.

Income alone doesn't explain everything — but it's a powerful predictor. Up next: education.
