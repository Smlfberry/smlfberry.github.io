The same counties, again

The low-turnout counties from the income chapter look familiar here too. These communities are being flagged by multiple indicators at once.

03 of 04

More degrees, more votes

Sorting by the share of adults with a college degree shows nearly the same staircase pattern as income. Counties in the top education quartile vote at rates roughly 29 points higher than counties in the bottom.

04 of 04

Education predicts turnout too

The scatterplot confirms it — and the slope is steep. Even controlling for income, education adds independent explanatory power.
