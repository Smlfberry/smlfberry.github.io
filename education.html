Where do low income and low education overlap?

Illinois counties with low income and low education are not randomly distributed and have significant overlap.

14 counties have both low income and low college attainment — are these the lowest turnout counties too?

02 of 04

More degrees, more votes

Sorting counties by share of college-educated residents shows a clear staircase — the more educated the county, the higher its turnout.

Counties where fewer than 18% hold a degree vote at markedly lower rates.

03 of 04

Education is a prevelant, yet weaker signal

The county-level scatter shows education and turnout are positively correlated. But, the relationship is less direct than income.

While education matters, it would appear that income is the more consistent predictor across Illinois counties.

04 of 04

Interestingly, these do not have a compounding effect

Counties with both low income and low education don't have the lowest turnout — counties with low education alone do.

This suggests education may be an independent driver of turnout, not just a proxy for income.
